# Governance

retrostrap runs on the benevolent-maintainer model. One person decides, in public, with
reasons written down.

- **How decisions happen.** In issues, in the open, never in DMs. A breaking change
  gets an RFC-flavored issue that stays open at least two weeks before it's implemented.
  The Boards are the place to talk; the issue is the place to decide.
- **Disagreements** are argued on the merits and on the five laws. The maintainer breaks
  ties and writes down why.
- **The laws are the constitution.** A change that would break a law changes the law
  first ([docs/02](docs/02-design-language.md)), in public, or it doesn't happen.
- **Becoming a co-maintainer.** Sustained, quality contributions over time, fluency in
  the laws, and review manners per the [code of conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), then an
  invitation from the maintainer. 2FA required; npm publish rights stay with the
  maintainer and are 2FA-protected.

This document is short on purpose. If it ever needs to be long, something has gone wrong.
